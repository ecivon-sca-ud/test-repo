Meal #1: Kaimati

Kaimati are like mandazi, but smaller and often sweeter.
First,here are the ingredients you need:

For the Kaimati Mix

  • 1 cup All Purpose Plain Flour


  • 1 tsp Active Yeast


  • 1/2 tsp Granulated Sugar


  • 1/2 cup Water (or as needed)


    • For the Sugar Syrup

  • 1/2 cup Granulated Sugar


  • 1/4 cup Water



Now to the steps in making them:

  1. In a mixing bowl, activate the yeast. Mix the yeast with 2 tbsp water and let it rest for 10 minutes. Add the yeast into the flour and mix. Add the rest of the ingredients and mix. Then add the water, little by little, until you have a sticky consistency (neither as thick as a dough nor very runny).


  2. Once done, cover the bowl with a cling film and let it rest in the oven for an hour (or a warm place if you live in a warm environment).


  3. After an hour, the mixture should have doubled in size. Using a spoon mix the mixture to remove all bubbles.


  4. Heat oil in a deep fryer, in high heat. Using the tip of your fingers, scoop a small amount of the kaimati mix and drop it in the hot oil. You need to drop it as close to the pan as you can. Careful. Dip your fingers in water and repeat the process. Do not overfill the fryer. Lower the heat to medium.


  5. Using a perforated spoon, keep moving in a clockwise motion to make sure the dough balls cook evenly on all sides. Once you have a good colour, remove the dough balls and let it rest.


  6. To make the sugar syrup, in another sauce pan add the rest of the sugar and water and let it come to a boil. Don't over boil it. You want a slightly sticky syrup. Let the sugar cool down completely.


  7. Serve both the Kaimati and the sugar syrup separately (like in the picture), or drop the kaimatis into the sugar syrup.

Meal #2: Beef Stew

An excellent and filling meal to make at all times,but even more so on a cold day!

Here are the ingredients needed:

  • 500g beef.


  • 2 tbsp olive oil


  • 2 tbsp fresh garlic cloves, minced


  • 1 cup yellow or red onion, diced


  • 2 tsp. curry powder mix (optional)


  • 2 Royco Beef Cubes


  • 1 cup diced/crushed tomatoes


  • 1 cup water


  • Salt and pepper to taste


  • 3 tbsp. fresh coriander, finely chopped salt




Now then, let's get into the process:

  1. Heat a large pan on medium heat.


  2. Add olive oil and onions then cook until soft.


  3. Next add garlic and spices, stirring constantly to prevent any sticking.


  4. Put in the beef and crumbled Royco Beef Cubes Beef Cubes cook until fragrant, then add tomatoes and water.


  5. Bring to a slight boil, turn heat down to low setting and simmer for about 30 minutes.


  6. Remove from heat and set aside until ready to serve.
